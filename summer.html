Why summer readiness

Do not wait until September to find the gaps.

Physics and calculus often feel difficult because students are asked to solve multi-step problems before their foundations are strong. Summer is the best time to build those foundations before the pace of the school year accelerates.

Your student is entering physics, AP Physics, or AP Calculus next year.
They are strong but need more structure before a demanding course.
They tend to memorize formulas without knowing when to use them.
They understand examples in class but struggle on tests.
They want to start the year with confidence instead of playing catch-up.

Flagship summer courses

Choose the right readiness path.

Each course is designed around first-principles instruction, structured practice, and the reasoning habits students need in demanding physics and calculus courses.

High school physics

High School Physics Readiness

Best for students entering regular physics, honors physics, or students who want a stronger foundation before AP Physics.

A foundation-building course for students who want physics to make sense from the beginning, not after they are already behind.

  • Units and dimensional reasoning
  • Graphs and data interpretation
  • Motion and kinematics
  • Forces and free-body diagrams
  • Energy and momentum foundations
  • Problem-solving structure
  • Translating word problems into diagrams and equations

AP Physics

AP Physics Readiness

Best for students entering AP Physics 1, AP Physics 2, AP Physics C Mechanics, or AP Physics C Electricity & Magnetism.

A first-principles summer intensive for students preparing for the pace, abstraction, and problem-solving demands of AP Physics.

  • Conceptual reasoning
  • Free-body diagrams
  • Kinematics and dynamics
  • Energy and momentum
  • Rotation foundations where appropriate
  • Circuits and E&M foundations where appropriate
  • AP-style problem solving
  • Explaining reasoning clearly
  • Avoiding formula memorization traps

AP Calculus

AP Calculus Readiness

Best for students entering AP Calculus AB or AP Calculus BC.

A summer readiness course for students who want to enter AP Calculus with stronger algebra, function, graphing, and conceptual foundations.

  • Function fluency
  • Algebra and trigonometry review
  • Limits and continuity preview
  • Derivative intuition
  • Rate-of-change thinking
  • Graph interpretation
  • Calculator and non-calculator reasoning
  • Problem-solving habits for AP-style questions

Summer FAQ

Summer course questions

What summer courses are available?

The flagship summer courses are High School Physics Readiness, AP Physics Readiness, and AP Calculus Readiness. Algebra 1, Algebra 2, Geometry, and Precalculus support may be available upon request.

When are the summer courses offered?

Courses are offered in July and August, with the summer session running July 6 – August 28 depending on the course.

How long are the summer courses?

Each Summer Readiness Course is 8 weeks.

How much does a summer course cost?

The course is $699 and includes 8 weeks of small-group instruction plus 3 private support hours.

Are the summer courses online or in person?

Courses are available online, with local Howard County availability where applicable.

How are schedules determined?

Course schedules will be finalized based on student availability and enrollment.

Are these private or group courses?

Summer Readiness Courses are small-group intensives. Each course also includes 3 private support hours for individualized help.

Do summer courses guarantee a better grade?

No. Axiom Scholars does not guarantee grades or scores. The courses are designed to build stronger foundations, preview important concepts, and improve problem-solving habits before the school year begins.
