AP Physics Readiness

Best for students entering AP Physics 1, AP Physics 2, AP Physics C Mechanics, or AP Physics C Electricity & Magnetism.

A first-principles summer intensive for students preparing for the pace, abstraction, and problem-solving demands of AP Physics.

  • Conceptual reasoning
  • Free-body diagrams
  • Kinematics and dynamics
  • Energy and momentum
  • Rotation foundations where appropriate
  • Circuits and E&M foundations where appropriate
  • AP-style problem solving
  • Explaining reasoning clearly
  • Avoiding formula memorization traps

A summer readiness course for students who want to enter AP Calculus with stronger algebra, function, graphing, and conceptual foundations.

  • Derivative intuition

  • Calculator and non-calculator reasoning
  • Problem-solving habits for AP-style questions

Why Axiom Scholars

Why Axiom Scholars?

Many capable students are taught to memorize formulas, procedures, and shortcuts before they understand the ideas. Axiom Scholars reverses that order: principles first, then structured practice, then confident performance.

First-principles teaching

Students learn what is happening beneath the equation so formulas become reasoning tools.

Personalized academic coaching

Sessions adapt to the student’s course, teacher expectations, timeline, goals, habits, and confidence level.

Structured practice

Practice is selected deliberately: conceptual, computational, exam-style, and teacher-assigned.

Clear progress plan

Parents and students understand what has improved, what remains difficult, and what comes next.

STEM foundation

The Axiom Method

A first-principles approach to STEM learning.

This is not casual homework help. The work begins by finding the precise gap, then builds a sequence of ideas the student can reason through under pressure.

  1. 01

    Diagnose the gap

    Identify whether the challenge is conceptual, mathematical, organizational, or test-strategy related.

  2. 02

    Build intuition

    Students learn what is physically or mathematically happening before they memorize procedures.

  3. 03

    Connect ideas to equations

    Equations become tools for reasoning, not random symbols to memorize.

  4. 04

    Practice realistic problems

    Students work through AP-style, honors-level, SAT/ACT-style, and teacher-assigned problems with feedback.

  5. 05

    Track progress

    Students and parents get a clear sense of what has improved and what comes next.

Do you offer summer courses?

What summer courses are available?
